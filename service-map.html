Service map

Ministry of Educational Transformation · Service-design artefact · Last updated 29 May 2026

A citizen-facing taxonomy for the Ministry of Educational Transformation's online forms. Groups today's 21 separate forms into 4 proposed services plus 7 stand-alone transactions, organised by the audience who uses them.

This is a working artefact — not a recommendation to implement as-drawn. See the caveats at the bottom of the page.

How to read this map

  • Cards tagged Proposed service are consolidated services — one front-door that branches into several form journeys for related needs.
  • Cards tagged Stand-alone form have no obvious consolidation partner today.
  • Each form name is clickable — it opens the existing prototype.

Citizen-facing taxonomy

P

If you're a parent

You're a parent or guardian of a child in or about to enter the Barbadian school system.

Proposed service · 1 of 4

School grants and bursaries

One application captures you, your child, and your bank details once. You then pick which awards you're applying for. Used at the start of each school year.

Branches into

  • $100 Textbook Grant — annual, per child
  • $100 Uniform Grant — annual, per child
  • Special Education Bursary — heavier flow, means-tested
Proposed service · 2 of 4

Your child's BSSEE

A single front-door for every BSSEE-related parent journey — from choosing schools through to requesting a reallocation after results.

Branches into

  • Choose secondary schools — standard path, all parents
  • Apply for an early sitting (Form A)
  • Defer the exam by one year (Form B)
  • Request access arrangements (Form C)
  • Request a reallocation after results
Stand-alone form

Apply to home-school your child

Different audience emotional state and long evidence-heavy flow — kept separate from the BSSEE service.

Stand-alone form

Apply for a Certificate of Exemption from Attendance at School

Education Act journey — medical, disability, relocation, religious, or home-schooling reasons. Signposts to the home-schooling application where relevant.

Stand-alone form

Apply for a non-national child to attend a government secondary school

Specific residency/legal shape — different supporting evidence and a different downstream team.

Stand-alone form

Apply for placement in a 6th Form school

Post-CSEC transition. Different lifecycle stage from the BSSEE service (older audience, different schools, CAPE subject selection). Could later pair with CXC private-candidate service if a student applies via both routes.

S

If you're a student (private candidate)

You're sitting a CXC examination outside of a school — either preparing privately for your first attempt, or resitting a subject.

Proposed service · 3 of 4

Register as a CXC private candidate (and claim refunds)

CSEC and CAPE registrations are almost identical forms today, and the refund flow shares the same audience and identity. One service covers the full registration-to-refund lifecycle for private candidates.

Branches into

  • Register for CSEC (January or May/June sitting)
  • Register for CAPE (May/June sitting)
  • Claim a refund of examination fees
Stand-alone form

Request an examination results statement

Broader audience than just private candidates — anyone who sat a CSEC, CAPE, BSSEE, or other exam through the Ministry. Distinct lifecycle (long after the exam) from the registration service.

T

If you're a teacher

You teach in (or are applying to teach in) a Barbadian school.

Proposed service · 4 of 4

Claim for exam work

A teacher invigilating CSEC in May claims both a duty payment and travel reimbursement for the same period. Today these are two forms with overlapping data — proposed as one combined monthly claim.

Branches into

  • Claim payment for examination duties
  • Claim examination travel expenses
Stand-alone form

Apply for the post of temporary teacher

A pre-employment journey, not an in-role transaction — different team, different timeline.

Stand-alone form

Apply for term's leave

Could later sit in a wider "Teacher administrative requests" service if more teacher forms emerge (sabbatical, secondment, retirement). For now, one form.

SS

If you're school staff

You're filling out something about a child in your care, not about yourself.

Stand-alone form

Refer a student to Student Support Services

The only form filled out by school staff about a child. Completely different audience and authentication needs from the parent-facing forms — kept separate.

Caveats — what this map doesn't yet show

A senior GDS service designer reviewed this map and pushed back on the following. Treat the map above as a hypothesis to test, not a plan to ship.

It's a form architecture, not a service map

A real service map includes discovery, eligibility, application, processing, outcome, payment, queries, and complaints — across all channels. This map covers the application step only.

No user research backs the audience taxonomy

"Parent / student / teacher / school staff" is an org chart, not a behavioural segmentation. A teacher whose child is also sitting BSSEE wears two of these hats at once.

No assisted-digital or offline channels

Every consolidation here is digital-first. The map says nothing about parents without smartphones, areas with intermittent broadband, or the in-person/phone fallback that the Ministry must still offer.

It's missing the back office

Some consolidations (Service 1) cross two very different back-office teams: the Grants Unit and the Special Education Section. The merge isn't deliverable until those teams' workflows can absorb it.

Identity is the hard problem, not a footnote

Real consolidation gains require a citizen identity layer ("MyGovBB") that pre-fills personal details. Without it, every saving here is marginal.

It doesn't show what's missing

The Ministry has off-system transactions today — update a child's school details, withdraw a child, request a transcript, appeal a Bursary decision. A real service map surfaces those gaps, not just the existing digitised forms.

Naming still uses internal jargon

"BSSEE" is the Ministry's acronym, not how every parent describes the eleven-plus / secondary entrance. User-facing labels need testing.

Recommended sequencing

If the proposed consolidations are pursued, do them in low-risk order:

  1. Service 3 first. CSEC + CAPE are ~95% the same form. Lowest similarity risk, easiest pilot of the consolidation pattern.
  2. Then the front half of Service 1. Textbook + Uniform Grants share audience, time of year, evidence, and payment. Keep the Special Education Bursary as a separate branch within the same service because of its weight.
  3. Then Service 2. Four BSSEE forms behind one triage. Retire "Form A/B/C" from the user-facing surface.
  4. Then Service 4. Exam-work claims — needs Exams Officer + DPS + Account Officer to agree the merged back-office workflow first.
  5. Eventually: the MyGovBB identity layer.
