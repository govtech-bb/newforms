Phase 1

The Problem

What problem were you trying to solve?

Problem statement

Describe the problem in one or two sentences. Who experiences it? What happens today?

Who does it affect?

Who are the people impacted? Citizens, staff, both? How many people are affected?

Why does it matter?

What is the impact? Wasted time, confusion, cost, poor outcomes?
Phase 2

Problem Framing

How did you define and scope the problem?

How we framed the problem

How did you narrow down the problem? What "How Might We" question did you use?

Assumptions we challenged

What did you assume at the start? What turned out to be wrong or more complex than expected?

Scope

What did you decide to focus on? What did you leave out of scope and why?
Phase 3

Journey / Service Mapping

What does the current experience look like?

The current journey

Walk us through the steps someone goes through today. What does the current process look like from start to finish?

Pain points we discovered

Where are the biggest frustrations? What takes too long, is confusing, or fails entirely?

Key insight

What was the most surprising or important thing you learned from mapping the journey?
Phase 4

Ideation

What ideas did you generate and how did you choose?

Ideas we explored

What ideas came up during brainstorming? List the main ones, even the wild ideas.

How we narrowed down

How did you decide which idea to take forward? What criteria did you use? (Feasibility, impact, user need?)

The idea we chose

Describe the idea you chose to prototype in a few sentences. Why this one?
Phase 5

Rapid Prototyping

What did you build and what decisions did you make?

What we built

Describe your prototype. What form did it take? (Paper, digital, screens, role play?) What does it do?

Key design decisions

What were the most important choices you made? Why did you design it this way?

What was hard

What was the trickiest part of building the prototype? What trade-offs did you make?
Phase 6

User Testing & Iteration

What did you test, learn, and change?

How we tested

Who did you test with? How did you run the test? What did you ask them to do?

What we learned

What worked well? What confused people? What surprised you?

How we iterated

What changes did you make based on testing? How is the final version different from the first?
Our Solution

Name of your solution

One sentence that explains what your solution does and for whom

What it does

How does it work? What does the user experience?

How it helps

How does it solve the problem? What improves for the user?

What makes it different

What is new or different about this approach compared to today?
Reflections

What We Learned & Next Steps

Key takeaways and what would come next

Biggest takeaway

What is the single most important thing your team learned through this process?

What surprised us

What did you not expect? What changed your thinking?

Next steps

If this project continued, what would you do next? More research, build a real version, test with more people?
