Stall requirements for a temporary restaurant

Last updated on July 2026

Use this guidance to set up a food stall that meets the standards an Environmental Health Officer will check. These are the basic requirements — an officer may give further instructions after inspecting your stall.

If anything is unclear, contact the polyclinic for the area where your event will be held.

The five keys to food safety

Clean. Separate. Cook. Chill. Use safe water.

  • Keep clean — wash hands before handling food and after using the toilet; wash and sanitise all surfaces and equipment; protect food from insects, pests and other animals.
  • Separate raw and cooked — keep raw meat, poultry and seafood away from other foods; use separate utensils and cutting boards; store food in containers to avoid cross-contamination.
  • Cook thoroughly — especially meat, poultry, eggs and seafood; heat food to at least 70°C; reheat cooked food thoroughly.
  • Keep food at safe temperatures — do not leave cooked food at room temperature for more than 2 hours; keep hot food above 60°C and cold food below 5°C; do not thaw food at room temperature.
  • Use safe water and raw materials — use safe water, or treat it to make it safe; choose fresh, wholesome food; wash fruit and vegetables; do not use food beyond its expiry date.

Your stall — construction and layout

  • Walls and a roof, with at least three sides, made of smooth, clean, impervious material to keep out rain, dust, leaves and pests.
  • Flooring that is smooth, clean, impervious and sloped for drainage.
  • Tables and counters that are smooth, clean and in good repair.
  • Good natural or artificial lighting.
  • Keep food and equipment off the ground — at least 18 inches — and well covered.
  • Protect displayed food with sneeze guards, and keep separate areas for food preparation (for example, grills and raw-food prep).
  • Keep the stall clean, tidy and protected from pests.

Handwashing and washing facilities

  • A handwashing container of at least 20 litres, with a tap and potable, warm running water, plus liquid soap and paper towels.
  • Separate stations for handwashing and for washing utensils and food.
  • Separate basins for washing and rinsing utensils and equipment, with hot water and/or a food-grade sanitiser.
  • A waste-water container, emptied at an approved location.
  • Keep facilities clean, in good repair and easy to reach — and wash hands properly and often.

Keeping food hot and cold

  • Keep cold food at 5°C or below — use coolers with ice, or refrigerators.
  • Keep hot food at 60°C or above — use chafing dishes, hot boxes or warmers; stir food and check the temperature.
  • Two-hour rule: do not leave food that can spoil at room temperature (5°C to 60°C) for more than 2 hours.

Preventing contamination

  • Keep raw and ready-to-eat food apart, and cover food to protect it from dust, insects and the sun.
  • Use tongs or utensils for ready-to-eat food — avoid handling it with bare hands.
  • Store cleaning chemicals away from food.
  • Get all food from a safe source.

Waste

  • A covered, leak-proof bin at all times.
  • A plan to remove waste and waste-water from the site.

Staff health and personal hygiene

All food handlers need a valid medical certificate for the current year, must have it with them at the event, and must know how to handle food safely.

Always:

  • Wear a hair restraint and keep hair covered.
  • Wear clean clothes and an apron.
  • Keep nails short and clean.
  • Wash hands regularly and properly.
  • Cover all wounds with waterproof dressings.
  • Use utensils (tongs, spoons) to handle ready-to-eat food.
  • Stay home if you are sick — for example, with vomiting, diarrhoea, fever or a sore throat.

Never:

  • Wear earrings, necklaces, chains, watches, rings or bracelets.
  • Keep items in outer pockets.
  • Cough, sneeze or talk over food.
  • Eat, drink, smoke or chew gum in food areas.
  • Handle money and food at the same time.
  • Use a mobile phone in food-preparation areas.

Toilets

  • Access to toilet facilities for staff, with handwashing nearby.

What your site or stall plan should show

  • stall positions
  • water points
  • handwashing and washing stations
  • waste storage areas
  • toilets

What the Environmental Health Officer will check

  • Stall construction and cleanliness
  • Handwashing and washing facilities
  • Food storage and temperature control
  • Food handling and personal hygiene
  • Waste management
  • Licences and health certificates

Apply at least two weeks (14 days) before your event. Display your licence in the stall, or have it available on request, and follow all instructions from Environmental Health Officers.
