Stall requirements for a temporary restaurant

Last updated on July 2026

Use this guidance to set up a food stall that meets the standards an Environmental Health Officer will check before your event. It also shows what your site or stall plan should include.

If anything is unclear, contact the polyclinic for the area where your event will be held.

Safe water

  • A supply of safe, potable water for preparing food, cleaning and handwashing.
  • If there is no mains connection, bring enough stored water in clean, covered containers.

Handwashing

  • A dedicated handwashing station with soap and paper towels, separate from where you wash food or equipment.
  • A way to catch and remove the used water.

Waste

  • Covered, leak-proof bins for food waste and rubbish.
  • A plan to remove waste and wastewater from the site.

Keeping food safe

  • Keep cold food cold and hot food hot. Bring a cooler or hot-holding equipment as needed.
  • Keep raw and ready-to-eat food apart, and cover food to protect it from dust, insects and the sun.
  • Avoid handling ready-to-eat food with bare hands.

Your stall

  • Overhead cover, and surfaces that are smooth and easy to clean.
  • Keep the stall clean, tidy and protected from pests.

Staff health and hygiene

  • All food handlers need a valid medical certificate for the current year, and must have it with them at the event.
  • Food handlers should wear clean clothing and must not work while unwell.

Toilets

  • Access to toilet facilities for staff, with handwashing nearby.

What your site or stall plan should show

  • stall positions
  • water points
  • handwashing stations
  • waste storage areas
  • toilets

An Environmental Health Officer will confirm what is needed for your event, and may inspect your set-up before the licence is issued.
