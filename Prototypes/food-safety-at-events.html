Food safety at events

Festivals, fairs and events mean food on every corner. Most food stalls are temporary restaurants, and there are rules that keep what you eat safe. Here is how to buy with confidence, and what to do if something is not right.

5 signs a stall is doing things right

  1. A licence on displayStalls should be able to show their temporary licence.
  2. A covered, well-built stallA roof and at least 3 sides, with clean, easy-to-clean surfaces.
  3. Food kept off the ground and coveredAt least 18 inches up, behind covers or sneeze guards.
  4. A handwashing station in useRunning water, soap and paper towels – and staff actually using them.
  5. Hot food hot, cold food coldHot food steaming (60°C or above), cold food properly chilled (5°C or below).

If you are buying food

Common questions
What counts as a temporary restaurant?

It is any food stall, booth, tent, table or bar serving food or drink to the public for a short time – up to 30 days. Most food vendors at Crop Over events are temporary restaurants.

Does a food stall need a licence?

Yes. Anyone serving food or drink to the public needs a temporary restaurant licence, whether they sell it or give it away – free samples included. The licence should be on display at the stall, so you can ask to see it.

How do I know the food is safe to eat?

Trust what you can see. Good signs are:

  • hot food kept steaming hot and cold food kept properly chilled
  • food covered and kept up off the ground
  • staff with clean hands and covered hair
  • raw and ready-to-eat food kept apart, with clean utensils

If food has been sitting out uncovered for a long time, it is safer to give it a miss.

What does a well-run stall look like?

It has a roof and at least 3 sides made from clean, easy-to-clean materials, a handwashing station with soap and water, separate areas for raw and ready-to-eat food, and covered bins for waste. These are the same things an environmental health officer checks before a licence is issued.

I am worried about a food stall – who do I tell?

Environmental health officers oversee food at events. You can report a concern to the Ministry of Health and Wellness, or to the polyclinic nearest the event. Contact details are at the bottom of this page.

If you are selling food

For vendors and organisers
Do I need a licence to sell food at an event?

Yes. Both the event organiser and each food vendor must apply for their own licence – for any food or drink, sold or given away. There is no application fee.

When should I apply?

Apply at least 14 days before your event. Applications made later than this may not be accepted. Crop Over ends on 4 August, so for this year's events the deadline has likely passed – but you can apply now for events later in the year, and it is worth applying early.
